# PEO-System


A Provincial Engineering Office (PEO) is a local government department responsible for planning, designing, constructing, and maintaining public infrastructure within a province. It operates under the authority of the Department of Public Works and Highways or the provincial government, depending on the structure of the area.

Main Functions of a Provincial Engineering Office:

Infrastructure Development
Oversees the construction and repair of provincial roads, bridges, government buildings, drainage systems, and other public facilities.

Project Planning and Design
Prepares engineering plans, cost estimates, feasibility studies, and technical specifications for infrastructure projects.

Maintenance and Rehabilitation
Ensures regular inspection and maintenance of existing infrastructure to guarantee public safety and usability.

Disaster Response and Recovery
Assists in clearing roads, repairing damaged structures, and restoring public facilities after natural disasters such as typhoons and earthquakes.

Technical Support to LGUs
Provides engineering assistance to municipalities and barangays within the province.

Regulatory and Safety Compliance
Ensures that public infrastructure projects follow national building codes, safety standards, and environmental regulations.
